The research question pursues the goal of understanding the digital balance of the Syrian diasporic refugees in Belgium. This is done firstly by establishing a detailed understanding of the Syrian diasporic refugees’ experience in relation to social media connectivity and dis-connectivity. In other words, their social media usage and their attempts to reduce it or to dis-connect from social media. Secondly, we seek to reveal the nature of their motives to connect to social media and whether their social media motives are ruled by intrinsic or extrinsic stimuli or a combination of both. Thirdly, we achieve this goal by critically reviewing and listening to their perception of digital well-being and digital balance, their tools and methods that help them to preserve a sense of digital well-being or digital balance. Finally, this research contributes to the understanding of Syrian diasporic refugees’ perceived digital balance by developing a more thorough examination of the emotional dimension of the social media connectivity paradox. Aiming at understanding people’s experiences, values and emotions requires the research to employ a qualitative research design by using in-depth interviews as a research method to examine the digital balance of Syrian diasporic refugees in Belgium.

Diaspora, home and belonging, three different concepts that are firmly relevant to each other in migration studies. In the current era of digital media, the prominent presence of social media among refugees in their everyday life and the impact social media has on refugees has attracted many scholars. Many studies have analysed the impact of social media in relation to home, belonging and refugees diaspora. However, this article concentrates on the role of social media influencers (SMIs) in the feeling of belonging, refugees diaspora, and home, which is hardly investigated in the literature, on the basis of a qualitative approach that targets Syrian SMIs who belong to the Syrian refugee diaspora. This paper attempts to develop a comprehensive understanding of how Syrian SMIs can contribute to the feeling of belonging, home and refugees diaspora. Thus, an analysis of Syrian SMIs is offered, while examining their practices and strategies that benefit Syrians in the feeling of belonging, home and the refugees diaspora.

This paper addresses questions such as: How do Syrians as transnational families’ members integrate social media into their lives as a communicative tool? What value do they see in it? What feelings are built around it? Is it helping them to bridge the distance? Which applications and functions do they use? To find answers to the research question of this paper, as well as other related questions, a qualitative research method was used. Twelve Syrians living in Belgium were interviewed online via the Zoom application. In conducting the interviews, forming the questions and structure, and coding and analysing the data, grounded theory was the framework used in this research.
